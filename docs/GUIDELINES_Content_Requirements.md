# Content Generation Guidelines & Requirements

## Workflow Requirements

The workflow must:
- **Be repeatable/sustainable** - Work for any feature launch, not just this one
- **Minimize human intervention** - Automated with minimal manual touchpoints
- **Have appropriate tone of voice** - Match audience expectations
- **Be suitable for the medium/channel** - Follow platform-specific best practices
- **Support engagement goals** - Drive excitement and action for the new feature

---

## Target Audience

- **Role**: Executives in businesses of ~4-50 people
- **Background**: Not legally trained
- **Goal**: Minimize time and money spent on legal activities
- **Needs**: Reliable and accessible solutions
- **Location**: US-based

---

## Channel-Specific Requirements

### LinkedIn Post

**Format:**
- **Length**: 150-300 words (short & punchy)
- **Hook**: First line must grab attention immediately
- **Tone**: Professional but conversational
- **Structure**: Short lines with line breaks for mobile readability
- **Call-to-action**: Clear CTA at the end
- **Hashtags**: 3-5 relevant hashtags
- **Paragraphs**: No long blocks of text - people scroll fast

**Best Practices:**
- Start with a pain point or question
- Use emojis sparingly (1-2 max) for visual breaks
- Keep sentences short and punchy
- Make it shareable - content colleagues would forward
- Include a direct link or CTA

**Example Structure:**
```
[Pain point/Hook - 1 line]

[Relatable scenario - 2-3 lines]

[Solution introduction - 1-2 lines]

[How it works - 3 bullet points or short lines]

[Benefit/outcome - 1-2 lines]

[CTA]

#Hashtag #Hashtag #Hashtag
```

## Engagement & Excitement Tactics

### 1. Create Emotional Connection
- Start with frustration/pain points they recognize
- Use customer quotes and real scenarios
- Show empathy: "We've all been there..."

### 2. Quantify Benefits
- "Save 5 hours per contract review"
- "Reduce legal spend by 50%"
- "Review contracts in minutes, not hours"

### 3. Use Social Proof
- "Founders are already saying..."
- "Companies using this report..."
- Real customer quotes from feedback documents

### 4. Create Urgency/FOMO
- "Join companies already using this"
- "Available now for Pro Plan users"
- "Start saving time today"

### 5. Make It Actionable
- Clear, single call-to-action
- Remove friction: "Try it free"
- Direct links to product

### 6. Power Words for Engagement
- **Speed**: instant, immediately, in seconds, fast
- **Ease**: effortless, simple, easy, automatic
- **Impact**: game-changer, revolutionary, finally
- **Trust**: reliable, accurate, deterministic, professional
- **Value**: save, free, reduce, eliminate

---

## Tone of Voice Guidelines

### DO:
- Use simple, clear language (no legal jargon)
- Write conversationally, like explaining to a friend
- Focus on benefits, not features
- Address pain points directly
- Use "you" and "your" (reader-focused)
- Be confident but not arrogant
- Show understanding of their challenges

### DON'T:
- Use complex legal terminology
- Oversell or use hype
- Make it sound like a legal opinion tool
- Focus on technical implementation details
- Use passive voice excessively
- Write long, complex sentences
- Assume legal knowledge

## Content Quality Checklist

Before finalizing any content, verify:

- [ ] Addresses specific audience pain points
- [ ] Uses appropriate tone for the channel
- [ ] Follows format guidelines for the medium
- [ ] Includes clear, singular CTA
- [ ] Written at accessible reading level (no jargon)
- [ ] Has engaging hook/opening
- [ ] Quantifies benefits where possible
- [ ] Includes social proof or examples
- [ ] Creates excitement/curiosity
- [ ] Drives action (not just awareness)
