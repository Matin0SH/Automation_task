# BLOG POST JUDGE PROMPT

## TASK
Evaluate the blog post for quality, effectiveness, and adherence to B2B SaaS content marketing best practices.

## EVALUATION CRITERIA

Score each criterion out of 10, then calculate the average:

### 1. TITLE EFFECTIVENESS (10 points)
- **Length**: 50-80 characters (SEO-optimized)
- **Keyword Inclusion**: Contains feature name or primary keyword
- **Value Proposition**: Promises clear benefit
- **Compelling**: Creates curiosity without clickbait
- **Power Words**: Uses action words (Introducing, Transform, Simplify)

**Scoring Guide**:
- 9-10: Perfect length, SEO-optimized, highly compelling, clear value
- 7-8: Good title with minor improvements possible
- 5-6: Acceptable but could be more engaging or SEO-friendly
- 3-4: Weak, too long/short, or missing key elements
- 1-2: Poor title unlikely to drive clicks or rank

### 2. OPENING HOOK STRENGTH (10 points)
- **Problem-Focused**: Starts with relatable pain point or challenge
- **Audience Relevance**: Speaks directly to target reader's experience
- **Engagement**: Grabs attention and builds tension
- **Specificity**: Uses concrete scenarios, not generic statements

**Scoring Guide**:
- 9-10: Immediately engaging, highly relatable, builds strong tension
- 7-8: Good hook with room for more specificity
- 5-6: Decent opening but could be more compelling
- 3-4: Generic or weak connection to audience
- 1-2: Fails to engage or irrelevant

### 3. PROBLEM EXPLANATION DEPTH (10 points)
- **Thorough**: Adequately explores the challenge
- **Empathetic**: Shows understanding of audience struggles
- **Evidence-Based**: References customer feedback or real scenarios
- **Builds Case**: Creates need for the solution

**Scoring Guide**:
- 9-10: Thorough, empathetic, well-evidenced problem exploration
- 7-8: Good explanation with minor gaps
- 5-6: Adequate but could be deeper or more specific
- 3-4: Superficial or disconnected from audience reality
- 1-2: Poor or missing problem explanation

### 4. SOLUTION CLARITY (10 points)
- **Clear Introduction**: Feature presented simply and understandably
- **Outcome-Focused**: Emphasizes results, not technical specs
- **Accessible Language**: No jargon, easy for non-technical readers
- **Vision Casting**: Paints picture of improved workflow

**Scoring Guide**:
- 9-10: Crystal clear, outcome-focused, accessible, inspiring
- 7-8: Clear with room for simplification
- 5-6: Understandable but somewhat technical or vague
- 3-4: Confusing or too feature-focused
- 1-2: Unclear or inaccessible solution explanation

### 5. BENEFITS COMMUNICATION (10 points)
- **Quantified**: Uses specific numbers (hours saved, efficiency %)
- **Tangible**: Benefits are concrete and measurable
- **Prioritized**: Most important benefits come first
- **Comprehensive**: Covers 4-6 key benefits adequately

**Scoring Guide**:
- 9-10: Specific, quantified, prioritized, comprehensive benefits
- 7-8: Good benefits with room for more quantification
- 5-6: Benefits present but vague or under-explained
- 3-4: Weak or feature-focused instead of benefit-focused
- 1-2: Missing or poorly communicated benefits

### 6. STRUCTURE & SCANNABILITY (10 points)
- **Headers**: Uses H2/H3 subheadings effectively
- **Short Paragraphs**: 3-4 sentences maximum
- **Bullet Points**: Benefits and lists properly formatted
- **White Space**: Adequate breaks between sections
- **Logical Flow**: Follows Hook → Problem → Solution → Benefits → CTA

**Scoring Guide**:
- 9-10: Perfectly scannable, well-structured, easy to navigate
- 7-8: Good structure with minor formatting improvements
- 5-6: Acceptable but could use better headers or breaks
- 3-4: Dense or poorly organized
- 1-2: Wall of text, poor structure

### 7. USE CASES & SOCIAL PROOF (10 points)
- **Specific Scenarios**: Provides 1-2 concrete use cases
- **Authentic Quotes**: Uses real customer feedback
- **Relatable**: Examples resonate with target audience
- **Credible**: Trust-building elements present

**Scoring Guide**:
- 9-10: Specific, authentic, relatable use cases with strong proof
- 7-8: Good examples with room for more specificity
- 5-6: Has use cases but generic or weak
- 3-4: Vague or unconvincing examples
- 1-2: Missing use cases or social proof

### 8. CALL-TO-ACTION EFFECTIVENESS (10 points)
- **Clarity**: Single, specific action to take
- **Frictionless**: Easy to execute, no barriers
- **Visibility**: Stands out clearly in the post
- **Action Language**: Uses compelling verbs

**Scoring Guide**:
- 9-10: Crystal clear, compelling, easy CTA
- 7-8: Good CTA with minor improvements possible
- 5-6: CTA present but could be clearer
- 3-4: Vague or multiple competing CTAs
- 1-2: Missing or ineffective CTA

### 9. TONE & VOICE (10 points)
- **Professional**: Establishes authority and credibility
- **Conversational**: Accessible, not overly academic
- **Educational**: Teaches, doesn't just sell
- **Consistent**: Maintains tone throughout

**Scoring Guide**:
- 9-10: Perfect balance of professional and conversational
- 7-8: Good tone with occasional lapses
- 5-6: Mostly appropriate but some inconsistency
- 3-4: Too formal, too casual, or salesy
- 1-2: Inappropriate or inconsistent tone

### 10. CONTENT LENGTH & COMPLETENESS (10 points)
- **Word Count**: 400-800 words (comprehensive yet focused)
- **Completeness**: Covers all necessary elements
- **Conciseness**: No unnecessary fluff
- **Pacing**: Flows smoothly from section to section

**Scoring Guide**:
- 9-10: Perfect length, complete, concise, well-paced
- 7-8: Good length with minor wordiness or gaps
- 5-6: Acceptable but could be tighter or more complete
- 3-4: Too long/short or poorly paced
- 1-2: Significantly over/under word count

## RED FLAGS (Automatic Quality Failure)

If ANY of these are present, the blog post fails quality check:

1. **Legal Advice Implication**: Sounds like providing legal counsel
2. **Overpromising**: Unrealistic claims about capabilities
3. **Jargon Overload**: Technical/legal terms without explanation
4. **Missing CTA**: No clear next step
5. **No Customer Voice**: Doesn't reference customer feedback
6. **Feature Dump**: Lists specs without explaining benefits
7. **Corporate Buzzwords**: Overuse of "synergy", "leverage", etc.
8. **Poor SEO**: Title too long/short or keyword-stuffed
9. **Too Short**: Under 300 words (not comprehensive)
10. **Too Long**: Over 1000 words (loses reader attention)

## SCORING & PASS/FAIL

### Calculate Final Score:
1. Score each of the 10 criteria (1-10 points each)
2. Calculate average: (Sum of all scores) / 10
3. Round to nearest whole number

### Pass/Fail Criteria:
- **PASS**: Score ≥ 7 AND no red flags present
- **FAIL**: Score < 7 OR any red flag present

- Explain why these work for the target audience

### WEAKNESSES

- Provide concrete examples
- Explain impact on reader engagement

- Be specific with examples
