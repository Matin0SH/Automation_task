# NEWSLETTER EMAIL JUDGE PROMPT

## TASK
Evaluate the newsletter email for quality, effectiveness, and adherence to B2B SaaS email marketing best practices.

## EVALUATION CRITERIA

Score each criterion out of 10, then calculate the average:

### 1. SUBJECT LINE EFFECTIVENESS (10 points)
- **Length**: 50-80 characters (optimal for mobile preview)
- **Clarity**: Immediately communicates value or sparks curiosity
- **Action-Oriented**: Uses verbs or poses compelling questions
- **No Spam Triggers**: Avoids excessive caps, exclamation marks, or "FREE!!!"
- **Relevance**: Directly relates to the feature being announced

**Scoring Guide**:
- 9-10: Perfect length, highly compelling, clear value prop, no spam triggers
- 7-8: Good length and clarity, minor improvements possible
- 5-6: Acceptable but could be more engaging or clearer
- 3-4: Too long/short, vague, or contains spam triggers
- 1-2: Fails multiple criteria, unlikely to drive opens

### 2. OPENING HOOK STRENGTH (10 points)
- **Empathy**: Acknowledges a real pain point the audience faces
- **Relatability**: Uses scenarios the target audience experiences
- **Engagement**: Grabs attention within first 2 lines
- **Conversational Tone**: Sounds human, not corporate

**Scoring Guide**:
- 9-10: Immediately relatable, empathetic, engaging, conversational
- 7-8: Good hook with minor tweaks needed
- 5-6: Somewhat engaging but could be stronger
- 3-4: Generic or doesn't connect with audience pain points
- 1-2: Weak, corporate, or irrelevant opening

### 3. VALUE PROPOSITION CLARITY (10 points)
- **Clear Benefits**: Feature benefits are obvious within first 3 sentences
- **Outcome-Focused**: Emphasizes what users achieve, not feature specs
- **Quantifiable**: Uses specific numbers (time saved, efficiency gained)
- **Simple Language**: No jargon, accessible to non-technical readers

**Scoring Guide**:
- 9-10: Crystal clear value, quantified benefits, simple language
- 7-8: Clear value with room for more specificity
- 5-6: Value present but could be clearer or more quantified
- 3-4: Vague benefits or too technical
- 1-2: Value proposition unclear or missing

### 4. STRUCTURE & SCANNABILITY (10 points)
- **Short Paragraphs**: 2-3 lines maximum per paragraph
- **Line Breaks**: Adequate white space between sections
- **Bullet Points**: Benefits listed clearly with visual breaks
- **Mobile-Friendly**: Easy to read on small screens
- **Logical Flow**: Hook → Context → Solution → Benefits → Proof → CTA

**Scoring Guide**:
- 9-10: Perfectly scannable, mobile-friendly, logical flow
- 7-8: Good structure with minor formatting improvements
- 5-6: Acceptable but could use better breaks or bullets
- 3-4: Dense paragraphs, hard to scan
- 1-2: Wall of text, poor structure

### 5. SOCIAL PROOF & CREDIBILITY (10 points)
- **Authentic Quotes**: Uses real customer feedback from source documents
- **Relatable Testimonials**: Quotes resonate with target audience
- **Specific Use Cases**: Shows real-world application
- **Trust Signals**: Includes credibility markers (beta users, actual customers)

**Scoring Guide**:
- 9-10: Authentic, relatable quotes with strong credibility
- 7-8: Good social proof, could be more specific
- 5-6: Has proof but generic or weak
- 3-4: Weak or unconvincing testimonials
- 1-2: Missing social proof or sounds fabricated

### 6. CALL-TO-ACTION EFFECTIVENESS (10 points)
- **Clarity**: Single, specific action to take
- **Frictionless**: Easy to execute, no barriers
- **Action-Oriented Language**: Uses verbs ("Try", "Get", "Start")
- **Visibility**: Stands out visually in the email
- **Urgency**: Creates gentle motivation to act now (without pressure)

**Scoring Guide**:
- 9-10: Crystal clear, single CTA, easy to act on, visible
- 7-8: Clear CTA with minor improvements possible
- 5-6: CTA present but could be clearer or more compelling
- 3-4: Vague, multiple CTAs, or hard to find
- 1-2: Missing or ineffective CTA

### 7. TONE & VOICE AUTHENTICITY (10 points)
- **Conversational**: Sounds like a colleague, not a sales pitch
- **Personal**: Uses "you" and "we", includes personal touches
- **Helpful**: Positions feature as solving their problem
- **Human**: Avoids corporate buzzwords and marketing jargon
- **Consistent**: Maintains tone throughout

**Scoring Guide**:
- 9-10: Perfectly conversational, personal, helpful, authentic
- 7-8: Good tone with occasional lapses
- 5-6: Mostly conversational but some corporate language
- 3-4: Frequently sounds salesy or corporate
- 1-2: Completely corporate/salesy tone

### 8. AUDIENCE RELEVANCE (10 points)
- **Target Alignment**: Speaks directly to small business owners/founders
- **Pain Point Accuracy**: Addresses real challenges from customer feedback
- **Language Appropriateness**: Uses terms and examples audience understands
- **Context Awareness**: Shows understanding of their world

**Scoring Guide**:
- 9-10: Perfect audience alignment, highly relevant
- 7-8: Good relevance with minor misalignments
- 5-6: Generally relevant but could be more specific
- 3-4: Somewhat off-target or generic
- 1-2: Poor audience understanding

### 9. BENEFIT COMMUNICATION (10 points)
- **Time Savings Emphasized**: Clearly states hours/minutes saved
- **Ease of Use Highlighted**: Makes feature sound simple and accessible
- **Specific Outcomes**: Lists 3-5 concrete benefits
- **Prioritization**: Most important benefits come first
- **Tangible**: Benefits are real and measurable, not vague promises

**Scoring Guide**:
- 9-10: Specific, quantified, prioritized, tangible benefits
- 7-8: Good benefits with room for more specificity
- 5-6: Benefits present but vague or poorly prioritized
- 3-4: Weak or feature-focused instead of benefit-focused
- 1-2: Missing benefits or entirely feature-focused

### 10. EMAIL LENGTH & PACING (10 points)
- **Word Count**: 200-400 words (optimal for engagement)
- **Conciseness**: No fluff or unnecessary details
- **Pacing**: Moves reader smoothly from hook to CTA
- **Completeness**: Covers all necessary points without overwhelming

**Scoring Guide**:
- 9-10: Perfect length, concise, well-paced, complete
- 7-8: Good length with minor wordiness
- 5-6: Acceptable but could be tighter or more complete
- 3-4: Too long/short or poorly paced
- 1-2: Significantly over/under word count, poor pacing

## RED FLAGS (Automatic Quality Failure)

If ANY of these are present, the email fails quality check regardless of score:

1. **Legal Advice Implication**: Sounds like providing legal counsel or replacing lawyers
2. **Overpromising**: Makes unrealistic claims about the feature
3. **Spam Triggers**: Subject line uses "FREE!!!", "ACT NOW!!!", excessive caps
4. **Jargon Overload**: Uses technical or legal terms without explanation
5. **Missing CTA**: No clear next step for the reader
6. **No Customer Voice**: Doesn't reference any customer feedback or quotes
7. **Feature Dump**: Lists technical features without explaining benefits
8. **Corporate Speak**: Uses buzzwords like "synergy", "leverage", "best-in-class"
9. **Misleading Subject**: Subject line doesn't match email content
10. **Too Long**: Over 500 words (loses reader attention)

## SCORING & PASS/FAIL

### Calculate Final Score:
1. Score each of the 10 criteria (1-10 points each)
2. Calculate average: (Sum of all scores) / 10
3. Round to nearest whole number

### Pass/Fail Criteria:
- **PASS**: Score ≥ 7 AND no red flags present
- **FAIL**: Score < 7 OR any red flag present
