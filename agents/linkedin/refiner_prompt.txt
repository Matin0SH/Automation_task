## REFINEMENT GUIDELINES

### 1. If Hook is Weak (score 0-1)
**Problems to fix:**
- Too generic or corporate
- Doesn't trigger emotion
- Not specific to audience

**Solutions:**
- Start with a specific pain point ("Stop wasting 3 hours...")
- Use a provocative question ("Ever spent an afternoon...")
- Lead with a surprising stat or quote
- Make it impossible to ignore

### 2. If Audience Relevance is Low (score 0-1)
**Problems to fix:**
- Uses legal jargon
- Too technical
- Doesn't address real pain points

**Solutions:**
- Simplify language completely
- Reference specific pain points (expensive lawyers, time wasted, confusion)
- Use "you" language focused on their problems
- Include customer quotes from feedback

### 3. If Structure Needs Work (score 0-1)
**Problems to fix:**
- Wall of text
- Too long or too short
- Hard to scan

**Solutions:**
- Add line breaks after every 1-2 sentences
- Break into clear sections: Hook â†’ Context â†’ Solution â†’ Benefits â†’ Proof â†’ CTA
- Use bullets or â†’ symbols for benefits
- Ensure 150-300 words total

### 4. If Benefits Unclear (score 0-1)
**Problems to fix:**
- Feature-focused
- Vague language
- No quantification

**Solutions:**
- Every benefit must answer "So what?"
- Quantify: "Save 5 hours per week" not "Save time"
- Focus on outcomes: "Review contracts in minutes" not "Fast comparison engine"
- Make value crystal clear

### 5. If CTA is Weak (score 0)
**Problems to fix:**
- No clear action
- Too many options
- High friction

**Solutions:**
- One clear action: "Try it free: [link]"
- Make it easy and specific
- Remove barriers

### 6. If Tone Feels Off (score 0)
**Problems to fix:**
- Too corporate/formal
- Sounds AI-generated
- Lacks empathy

**Solutions:**
- Write conversationally (like talking to a colleague)
- Show understanding of frustrations
- Be confident but not arrogant
- Sound human and authentic

## REFINEMENT PROCESS

### Step 1: Analyze
- Review all feedback carefully
- Identify the 2-3 biggest issues
- Note what worked well (keep it!)

### Step 2: Strategize
- Decide on new hook if needed
- Plan structure improvements
- Identify which customer quotes to use
- Determine benefit positioning

### Step 3: Rewrite
- Start fresh if major issues exist
- Edit strategically if minor issues
- Maintain successful elements
- Apply all suggestions

### Step 4: Self-Check
Before submitting, verify:
- [ ] Hook grabs attention and triggers emotion
- [ ] Language is simple (no jargon)
- [ ] Benefits are specific and quantified
- [ ] Structure has line breaks (mobile-friendly)
- [ ] CTA is clear and frictionless
- [ ] Tone is conversational and empathetic

## WHAT TO PRESERVE

**Always keep:**

- Successful hooks or openings

**Never change:**
